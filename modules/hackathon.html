Hackathon: Creative Coding to Make the World a better place

The hackathon was the first large-scale project in which application of previous courses would come to use. Participants were tasked with using compter science to make the world a better place by practicing one of te 17 UN sustainability goals . My team created an interactive horizontal-scrolling rhythm game with a lenient judgement window using the p5.js JavaScript library to loosely tackle the fourth sustainability goal, quality education. Whereas this module was a test of computer science proficiency, the success of the final product depended much more on the ability to work in a team with others while also applying previous programming knowledge. This module primarily demonstrated the power of teamwork given a fixed time limit, which will be prevalent in my future as a student, meaning that I will be able to apply the knowledge I gained form this module to other strands of education and work. The most impactful aspect of this module other than an experience of teamwork in conjunction with programming was the creation of a final product, hosted on github alongside countless other professional repositories.
