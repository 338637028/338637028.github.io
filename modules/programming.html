Khan Academy AP®︎/College Computer Science Principles: Programming

The Programming unit of Khan Academy's computer science principle course gives students a brief yet intricate preview of various basic algorithms, variables and other elements common in many real programming languages. In the scope of the semester, this module serves as an excellent introduction to computer science concepts to new programmers and an engaging refresher of previous years for experienced coders, as this is the first module of the course. Whereas the unit does not iterate on many new or unfamiliar concepts, it serves as a common starting ground for students of varying experience before the many projects and new computer science concepts that await them in the coming semester. As this module taught very fundamental and broad concepts of programming, it will be very easy to apply the concepts taught from the module in various other strands of education, including basic logic, conditionals and mathematical operators. The most significant parts of this module included the concept of procedures and the basics of logical equivalence.
