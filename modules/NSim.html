Khan Academy Computer Programming Advanced JS: Natural Simulations

Khan Academy's Natural Simulations pushed the p5.js library to it's limit as it simulates various physics-heavy procedures and introduces advanced programming concepts such as new forms of random, object-oriented object-oriented design and noise. This module was the first real challenge of the course thanks to the many new concepts introduced, and thanks to the time assigned (i.e. at the very start of the semester along with other more simple modules) it serves as an excellent baseline of expectations for the coming projects. This module focuses specifically on spcific JS features as opposed to broad concepts that would be appliccable to other strands of education, however one could use the physics features of the p5.js library covered in this module to better their understanding of physics principles. The most impactful part of this module was the introduction of particle systems, storing large quantities of objects in single variables. This concept proved useful in subsequent projects, as being able to create large quantities of simple objects allows for much more diverse and complex sketches.
